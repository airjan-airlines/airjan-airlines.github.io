# content.md

## Site structure

Five main sections: Home, Work, Blog, Interests, Skills.

---

## Home

A hero with a short personal headline (not a job title), a one-liner on what
you're currently building, and 3-4 stat highlights pulled from your resume.
Two CTAs: one to Work, one to Blog. No navbar on load — just a scroll cue.

---

## Work

Split into two columns: Technical (left) and Non-Technical (right).

Technical is further split into Projects and Experience, navigable within
the column. Each project shows: what problem it solved, the approach, stack,
and outcomes. Each experience entry is a short narrative, not bullets.

Non-Technical covers creative/technical projects that aren't DS/AI work, plus
leadership roles. Framed around what they show about how you think, not as
extracurriculars.

A standalone Research subsection lives here too — your Harvard paper is a
major differentiator and should be surfaced prominently with a plain-language
abstract alongside the technical one.

---

## Blog

Index of posts with category (technical / essay), excerpt, and reading time.
Clean reading view. You should have at least one post live at launch.

---

## Interests

A small number of interest areas, each tied back to your work or thinking.
Not a hobbies list — every entry should answer "why does this matter to how
you build things."

---

## Skills

An interactive graph where nodes are skills, edges are relationships between
them, and clicking a node surfaces the projects/experiences where you used it.

---

## Supporting pages

- **Now** — what you're building, reading, and thinking about right now.
  Dated. Updated monthly.
- **Open to** — a specific, human statement of what collaborations or
  opportunities you're interested in. Not a hire-me banner.

---

## Cross-cutting rules

- Every section should link to evidence. Claims without proof get cut.
- The agent should pull all specific content (project names, outcomes,
  dates, organizations, stats) from the resume and documents in /context.
- No field should be written by the agent. Structured data only — the
  author fills in narrative fields.
